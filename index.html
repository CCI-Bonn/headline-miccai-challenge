01 — Overview

About the Challenge

Headline is a MICCAI 2026 challenge targeting automated radiology report generation from head CT imaging. Participants are invited to develop models that produce accurate, clinically meaningful free-text reports from volumetric CT data — mimicking the workflow of a practising neuroradiologist.

The challenge addresses a critical bottleneck in clinical care: the time-intensive nature of reporting. By benchmarking models across standardised tasks and metrics, Headline aims to accelerate progress toward practical, deployable reporting systems.

03 — Data & Tasks

Dataset & Tasks

The Headline dataset consists of de-identified head CT volumes paired with expert radiologist reports. Scans cover a range of pathologies including haemorrhage, infarct, mass effect, and normal findings. Further details on acquisition parameters, institutions, and annotation methodology will be provided upon challenge launch.

TASK 01
Report Generation

Given a head CT volume, generate a full free-text radiology report covering findings, impression, and recommendations.

TASK 02
Findings Extraction

Structured extraction of key clinical findings from generated or reference reports — enabling downstream quantitative analysis.

04 — Evaluation

Evaluation Metrics

Submissions will be evaluated on a held-out test set using a combination of automated NLP metrics and expert clinical assessment. The primary ranking metric will be announced at challenge launch.

BERTScore

Semantic similarity between generated and reference reports using contextual embeddings.

BLEU / ROUGE

N-gram overlap metrics providing lexical similarity baselines.

RadGraph F1

Clinical entity and relation matching tailored to radiology report structure.

Clinical Accuracy

Expert radiologist assessment of clinical correctness on a subset of predictions.

07 — FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can participate?

Headline is open to all researchers and practitioners worldwide, including academic groups, industry teams, and individuals. Teams of any size are welcome.

Is there a fee to participate?

Participation is free of charge. Registration through Grand Challenge is required to access the data and submit predictions.

What computational resources will I need?

There are no enforced hardware requirements. Participants are responsible for their own compute. Guidance on baseline model requirements will be provided alongside the training data release.

Will training data be publicly released after the challenge?

Details on post-challenge data access will be announced at launch. We aim to support the community with long-term dataset access where possible.

Can I use external data or pre-trained models?

Rules regarding external data and pre-trained models will be specified in the challenge protocol document, to be released at launch.
