Challenge Rules

Headline MICCAI 2026 — Head CT Radiology Report Generation

1. Submission Process

Validation Phase

A dedicated Validation Set (distinct from the final hidden Test Set) will be released to participants in the same format as the test data, without the associated ground-truth. Participants will generate outputs for these validation cases and upload these outputs to the evaluation platform. The results will appear on a publicly available leaderboard.

Teams will be permitted to submit up to a total of 3 submissions in this phase.

Sanity Check

The submissions for the validation phase above will be based on submitting the generated reports for the validation cases. The final (single) submission for the test phase will be a single Docker container containing the complete inference pipeline, which will then be applied to the hidden test data on the evaluation platform. To ensure that your Docker container is working as expected, there will also be a lightweight technical validation phase. This sanity check will use a small subset of cases and is intended solely to verify that submitted Docker containers execute correctly within the platform environment. Performance on this subset will not be used for ranking.

Final Evaluation

Participants are required to package their inference models as Docker containers and upload them directly to the evaluation platform for the final evaluation phase. The platform's evaluation system will then automatically execute these containers on the hidden test data in a secure, isolated cloud environment.

All submissions must comply with the predefined runtime and hardware constraints. These will be shared in more detail in the coming days.

2. Evaluation

In the first stage, all models are assessed using a weighted composite score based on: BERTScore (F1), METEOR, ROUGE-L, and BLEU-4. In the second stage, the top-performing models are evaluated by board-certified radiologists using a Clinical Utility Score, focusing on clinical accuracy, completeness, and potential for harm.

The complete evaluation source code will be made publicly available in our GitHub repository. We will provide the raw scripts used to compute the ranking metrics to ensure full transparency.

3. Data Usage

The training data will be released under a Custom Data Usage Agreement (DUA). Access is granted solely for the purpose of this challenge. Participants must explicitly accept these terms before downloading the data. Redistribution, sharing, or re-hosting of the dataset is strictly prohibited.

4. Prizes

We plan to give prizes to the three top-performing teams, with the exact amounts to be confirmed.

To be eligible for the monetary prizes, participants must fulfil the following conditions:

  1. Open Source: The winning teams must make their code publicly available under a permissive open-source licence and provide instructions for reproducibility.
  2. Method Description: Submission of a high-quality method description paper for the challenge proceedings.
  3. Presentation: At least one team member must register for the MICCAI conference and present their solution during the challenge workshop.

5. Publications

Challenge Summary Paper

The organising team will coordinate a joint journal paper summarising the challenge design, data, and results.

  • The core organising team constitutes the lead authorship. Additionally, up to two representatives from each of the top-performing teams will be invited to contribute.
  • Co-authorship is contingent upon a timely and substantial contribution to the manuscript (e.g., providing detailed analysis of their specific method).

Participant Publications

  • All participants are invited and encouraged to submit a paper describing their method to the challenge's LNCS Proceedings.
  • Participants are free to publish their own methods separately at any time.
  • However, participants may not publish any paper that includes a comparative analysis of the official challenge results or the private test data until the official Challenge Summary Paper is published.

6. Reproducibility & Transparency

To foster reproducibility and scientific progress, we strongly encourage all participants to make their code publicly available.

To be eligible for monetary prizes and inclusion in the challenge summary paper, the top-performing teams must release their training and inference code under a permissive open-source licence (e.g., MIT, Apache 2.0) on a public repository (e.g., GitHub). The code must be documented sufficiently to allow the reproduction of the submitted results.

7. Eligibility & Conflicts of Interest

Members of the core organising team and their direct research groups are excluded from participating in the competition for monetary prizes to avoid any conflict of interest.
