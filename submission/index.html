Submission & Evaluation

Complete guidelines for submitting to the HEADLINE challenge, including our hybrid evaluation framework and computational requirements.

Ranking & Evaluation Framework

The challenge uses a two-stage hybrid ranking system to prioritise both linguistic quality and clinical safety. This ensures that top-ranking models are not only linguistically strong, but also clinically safe and meaningful.

Stage 1: Automated Screening

All submissions are ranked via a Weighted Composite Score SAuto:

  • BERTScore (70%): Measures semantic similarity using contextual embeddings.
  • Auxiliary metrics (30%): METEOR, ROUGE-L, and BLEU-4 to assess structural integrity.

The automated screening provides an initial ranking that reflects how well submissions capture the linguistic and semantic content of reference reports.

Stage 2: Expert Clinical Review

The top 5 teams advance to a manual review by board-certified radiologists who calculate a Clinical Utility Score SHuman. This score evaluates:

  • Attribution/Hallucination: Information not supported by the image.
  • Completeness: Missing key findings (omissions).
  • Safety: Potential for patient harm.

Expert review ensures that the top solutions are clinically sound and safe for potential real-world application.

Final Score Calculation

The final ranking is determined by:

Final Score = 0.5 × SAuto + 0.5 × SHuman

Important: If a report fails to be generated for a given case, the submission will be assigned a score of 0 for that case. It is critical that your submission gracefully handles all cases and generates output for every test image.

Submission Guidelines

Submission will be handled through headline26.grand-challenge.org, and final evaluation on the test set will use a Docker container of the algorithms submitted by participants. There are three separate submission phases:

1. Validation Phase

Participants will be supplied 500 validation cases without ground-truth reports. They will then generate reports for these cases and submit the predictions for evaluation. This will form an interim leaderboard during the challenge.

  • Validation submissions are limited to three per team.
  • Results from the validation phase can be used to refine your method before final submission.
  • The validation leaderboard provides real-time feedback on performance.

2. Sanity Check

To ensure that the final test phase runs smoothly, participants will be asked to submit a Docker container of their algorithm for an initial sanity check. The containers will then be executed on a single case for inference.

This step is important to check that your container runs without problems on the Grand Challenge infrastructure, given the computational constraints outlined below. We strongly recommend completing this step before final submission to avoid runtime failures.

3. Test Phase

Participants will submit a Docker container of their algorithm, which will be executed on all 500 test cases.

  • Each test case will be processed according to the computational constraints specified below.
  • Any cases where a report is not generated will receive a score of 0.
  • Final results will form the official challenge leaderboard.

Please ensure your submission complies with the computation requirements and that you submit your method for an initial 'sanity check' as described above.

Computation Requirements

Inference and evaluation will be run on Grand Challenge, and so submissions will be bound by associated computational constraints.

Hardware Specifications

  • Memory (RAM): 32 GB DRAM
  • GPU: Either NVIDIA T4 (16 GB VRAM) or A10G (24 GB VRAM)
  • Storage: Either 225 GB (with T4) or 450GB (with A10G)

Runtime Constraints

Maximum allowed runtime per case: 6 minutes

This time includes model loading, data loading and preprocessing, inference, and all associated post-processing and output generation.

If a case exceeds the 6-minute limit, the process will be terminated and that case will receive a score of 0.
